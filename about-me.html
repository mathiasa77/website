A Brief Biography

At an early age I found the notion of life and existance very peculiar. Without getting too existential, I believe science and academia to be our most trusted and tested mathods of discovering the world around us. I was fortunate enough to grow up in Mountain View, CA, down the street from NASA Ames Research Center. Going to school with not only the sons and daughters of Silicon Valley engineers but of NASA engineers nurtured an environment that valued education and STEM. A gift I would only come to appreciate as an adult.

As a high school student at Mountain View High I took advantage of the wide variety of unique classes offered like Engineering Technology I and II and Physics AP. Frankly, I spent the mahority of time on my engineering studies than anything else. Although having somewhat of a one track mind at the time, I knew college was an endevour I wanted to embark on. At the time of my graduation in 2017 I was savagely craving my independence, inevitably deciding to move to San Diego to begin college on my own.

It was in my second year of community college where I decided I wanted to investigate the mechanisms of nature further; declaring a major in physics. My fascination with the natural world only grew when I was accepted and transfered into the Univeristy of California, Berkeley, one of the best days if my life I might add. Here I was led on a wild-ride through the macro and quantum world, satiating mathematical appetite.

Aside from challenging coursework, I was a member of a rocket team that made it abundantly clear that I knew I wanted to position my career where my a lot of free time had gone to: space technology.

A central goal of mine is to further our understanding of space beyond this pale blue dot. I believe that through the research and development of space technologies we can further extend human presence in space, efforts of which I plan to contribute greatly throughout my life.

Currently I've just returned from 3 month long sabbatical around Europe and I am pursuing a career in Aerospace Engineering.  I will be pursuing a PhD in the former in the near future.

Early Life in Aquatics

In my early years I had a relatively active presence in my community. Around the age of 13 I started volunteering as a swim instructor at the City of Mountain View Eagle Pool, an experience that would lead to a part-time job as a lifeguard when I turned 15. Fast forward 4 years and here I found myself working as a headguard Head Lifeguard: managing a staff of 60 employees, resolving conflicts, and taking ownership of being a keyholder.

There are plenty of reasons why lifeguarding is a great job but some of the few really stood out to me. As a young person, being given such a large responsability, ensuring the safety of others, didn't weigh on me lightly. Besides having to maintain numerous CPR, advanced first-aid, and other related certifications, I had the responsability of organizing staff trainings, maintaining pool chemistry, and ensure a safe environment.

It also turns out lifeguarding was the perfect job for a student, something I found out in high school. I carried on lifeguarding throughout my coommunity college experience at the La Jolla Family YMCA in San Diego, working 20 hours a week and quickly getting promoted to head lifegaurd once again. By the time I was ready to transfer to a univerity I had been working as a lifeguard for about 7 years, and I was sad to say goodbye. All in all, it a central contributor to the adult I am today and I am immensely grateful for all the values and lessons it has tought me throughout the years.
