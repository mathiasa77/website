Research Experience

Undergrad Research Apprenticeship Program:

Theoretical and Applied Fluids Laboratory

Working under Professor Reza Alam Ph.D, my research focus was on static and dynamic stability analysis of a rigid, bouyant body in a fluid. Moreover, given a turbalant environment, how the design of a bouyant body could reduce pitch, raw, roll deflection from wave-like perturbations while maintaining center-of-mass parameters.

Undergrad Laboratory @ Berkeley:

Terrestrial Gamma-Ray Flash Model

My undergraduate research group decided to make a model predicting TGF events based on data collected by NASA's Fermi Gamma-Ray Space Satelite. Extensive research in atmosphere particle physics and high-energy interactions led us to begin the creation of a Monte-Carlo Simulation aimed at predicting future TGF events.

Some of my Favorite Classes:

PHYS 105 Analytical Mechanics

Some of my favorite topics in science were covered in this course, such as orbital mechanics, lagrangian mechanics, and chaos theory. Frankly, I find the optimization of energies in a system very satisfying.

MECENG 163 Fluid Mechanics

Similar to my chaos theory, the physics behind laminar and turbulant flows was a privilage to learn and apply in my research. I look forward to implimenting what I learned into my future career ventures. MATLAB, along with ANSYS, were used to model laminar flow characteristics for assignments.

PHYS 111A Instrumentation Laboratory

Circuit design related to experimental data collection was emphasized in this class. Fundamental analog curcuit design practices and advanced signal processsing logic were utilized throughout the course. Additionally, LabView, a graphical programming language was introduced, essential in professional research and developement fields.

PHYS 111B Advanced Experimental Lab.

In this course four chosen experiments were conducted throughout the semester: muon Lifetime decay, atomic spectroscopy, compton scattering (isotopes of radioactive Americium-241 were used, cool!), and optical pumping. A strong knowledge of statistical analysis methods in Python wre required to varify models with the large data sets collected.
