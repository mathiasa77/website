Film Photography

The underappreciated medium

Some say a picture is worth a thousand words, I'm not convinced thats always the case. A photograph's message is up to those who is those who critisize it, negative or positive. This variation in interpretion is why I find photography so interesting, and use it as a creative outlet. Film photography in particular, I find, to capture life in motion the best. Just like it's subjects, the outcome of a photograph can be blurry, grainy, and unintentional. This medium is what I find parallels a message the best.

Outdoors, Camping, & Overlanding

(a.k.a my happy place)

Living in a modern age surrounded by pixels and concrete tends to make one forget what true silence sounds like, what true darkness looks like, and these are the things I try not to forget. I love camping, and generally being in nature, because it reminds me of where I really live and serves as an environment to spend better quality time with those I care about.

Utah Overlanding 2022

"Overlanding or 4WD Touring is self-reliant overland travel to remote destinations where the journey is the principal goal." -Wikipedia

A 2 week trip took myself and some pals through all 5 national parks Utah had to offer. There's nothing like wheelin' in the dirt.

"While you can go fast, I can go anywhere"

Oregon Camping 2021

Who knew camping in the snow could be so refreshing! This trip took us through Northern California and the South-Eastern corner of Oregon, straight to the Alvord Desert Flat.

Along the way some natural hot springs were discovered off a trail, making me thankful for the 4WD Land Rover who got us there.

NorCal Land Rover Club

Oh so you want to see a group of grown adults nerd-out about some trucks? Join the NCLR Club!

In all seriousness, you can't find a nicer group of people wanting to have some honest fun in the dirt.

This is one way to spend a weekend!

International Travel

Lessons on Culture

I had the privilage of traveling to Europe once I graduated from university. For about two and a half months I lived in an international spanish language school where I got to meet students of all ages from all walks of life. The central lesson I learned while there was that it doesn't matter where you come from, everyone wants to be happy, they just have different ways of attaining it. Truly, a life-changing experience.

Barcelona, Spain

Being of Spanish and Mexican living in the US descent I wanted to learn about who I am when I visited Barcelona, Spain. Yes, it was partly a vacation, but for me it was a lot more.

It is said that when you understand a people's language you understand their culture. By the end of the trip I felt so much more pride in who I am and where I came from.

Paris, France

Visiting Paris definitely made the thought of going back to eating food in the Unites States a little more daunting. Not only did the food impress me greatly, but so did all the history and architecture! The passion and ferocity the French people demonstrate is something to admire, and strive after.

London, England

My time in London, England had me drawing a lot of parallels to the United States, considering I could comfortably order food at resturants! That's not to say it was bland, rather far from it. Something I really appreciated how welcoming people were; you'd meet someone and they'd just greet you with a joke as if they'd known you for years.

Rome & Naples, Italy

Oh how Rome, Italy took my breathe away! From the incredible pasta to the warm people, I was left jaw dropped everywhere we went. The small lively, slightly chaotic, streets makes you wonder how anyone can navigate it. It's not until you realize that everyone there seems to have a mutual-respect for each other, a common understanding of patience; a value I took with me.

Budapest, Hungary

Budapest, Hungary is extraordinarily undervalued when it comes to travel! Not only was there a church that shared my name, Matthias Church, but an amazingly diverse population. Spending only a couple days here visiting family friends left me yearning to explore more of the city and the people in it.
